# define: behaver-ergonomist relationship

## .what

the behaver declares behaviors. the ergonomist reviews whether those behaviors serve humans well.

behavers help declare the desired product, then construct it, then verify it, then deliver it. the factory.

ergonomists review behaviors from the human perspective. the products.

## .why

behavior is the core ergonomic concern — aesthetics is just the tip of the iceberg.

| concern | depth | question |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **behavior** | core | did we build what the user needs? |
| **friction** | middle | can they use it without pain? |
| **aesthetics** | surface | does it look/feel polished? |

## .the relationship

| role | focus | scope |
|------|-------|-------|
| **behaver** | the factory | declare, construct, verify, deliver |
| **ergonomist** | the products | human perspective on behaviors |

the behaver answers "what should we build?"
the ergonomist answers "did we build it empathetically?"

## .when each reviews

the ergonomist reviews the vision upstream — "will this serve humans well?"

by execution time, the behaver verifies intent coverage — "did the factory deliver what was declared?"

the ergonomist then reviews the product — "does it feel right to humans?"
