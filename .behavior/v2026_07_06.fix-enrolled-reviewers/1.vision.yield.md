# 1.vision — fix enrolled reviewers

## .what

teach every `rhx enroll claude` peer reviewer how to speak the review-output contract, and run them on a stronger brain.

two edits, applied to all 11 enrolled reviewers across the behavior init template guards:

1. add the bhrain `reviewer` role → `--roles ...,reviewer`
2. upgrade the brain → `--model sonnet` becomes `--model 'claude-sonnet-5[1m]'`

---

## the outcome world

### day-in-the-life

a driver drives a behavior route. it reaches an execution guard. level-3 peer reviewers spawn — each an enrolled claude that reviews the diff and prints a verdict. the route's judge (`route.stone.judge --mechanism reviewed?`) reads each reviewer's stdout to learn how many blockers and nitpicks it found.

- **before**: the enrolled brain writes prose like `no major blockers here` or `looks solid`. the judge finds no numeric count. per `contract.reviewer-output`, an unreadable review is a `💥 malfunction` — the stone blocks. the human is stuck babysitting a reviewer that did its job but couldn't be understood.
- **after**: the enrolled brain carries the `reviewer` role, whose `contract.reviewer-output` brief is loaded into its context. it knows to emit `0 blockers` / `2 nitpicks` as numbers. the judge parses cleanly. the stone advances or blocks for real reasons, not parse failures.

### before/after contrast

| aspect | before | after |
|--------|--------|-------|
| roles enrolled | `behaver,architect,mechanic` (or `…,ergonomist,…`) | same **+ `reviewer`** |
| brain | `--model sonnet` (whatever default sonnet resolves to) | `--model 'claude-sonnet-5[1m]'` |
| stdout | prose, often unparseable | numeric `N blockers` / `N nitpicks` |
| judge outcome | `💥 malfunction` on unreadable verdict | clean parse, real verdict |

### the "aha"

the fix is not "make the brain smarter about counting." it's "give the brain the role that already documents the contract." the knowledge already lives as a crystallized brief in bhrain's reviewer role — we just weren't enrolling it. one role slug closes the gap.

## user experience

### usecase

- **who**: the bhrain driver + judge system, running a behavior route's guard reviews.
- **goal**: get a parseable blocker/nitpick count from every enrolled peer reviewer so guards gate on real findings.

### contract inputs & outputs

the touched contract is the `run:` line of each enrolled peer reviewer inside the init template guards:

```yaml
# before
- slug: enroll-impl-behavior-intent
  run: $rhx enroll claude --model sonnet --roles behaver,architect,mechanic -p '…'
  budget: 3
  level: 3

# after
- slug: enroll-impl-behavior-intent
  run: $rhx enroll claude --model 'claude-sonnet-5[1m]' --roles behaver,architect,mechanic,reviewer -p '…'
  budget: 3
  level: 3
```

`--roles` accepts a comma list; enroll discovers linked roles by scanning `.agent/repo=*/role=*` and dedupes by slug (`invokeEnroll.ts:40-73`). `reviewer` lives at `.agent/repo=bhrain/role=reviewer/` and is already linked, so `,reviewer` resolves. enrolling it loads its `say` briefs — including `contract.reviewer-output.md` — into the brain's context.

### timeline

1. edit the 5 template guard files (11 `run:` lines)
2. future `init.behavior` copies the fixed templates into new routes
3. drivers spawn enrolled reviewers that know the contract and run on sonnet-5

- **analogy**: a competent inspector who wrote freeform notes the filing system couldn't index. we gave them the standard form to fill in. same inspection, now machine-readable.
- **terms they'd use**: "the reviews weren't parseable." **terms we use**: the reviewer stdout violated `contract.reviewer-output` (no numeric count for both dimensions).

## evaluation

### how well it solves the goal

directly. the root cause named in the wish is: enrolled brains produced reviews the judge/driver couldn't parse blocker counts from. the `reviewer` role's `contract.reviewer-output` brief states exactly the required stdout shape (numeric `N blockers` + `N nitpicks`). enrolling the role puts that contract in the brain's context. the sonnet-5 upgrade is an orthogonal quality bump the wisher asked for alongside.

### pros

- fixes at cause: enrolls the role that owns the contract, rather than patching prompts ad hoc
- consistent: one uniform edit across all 11 reviewers
- future-proof: templates are the source of truth; every new route inherits the fix
- low blast radius: only `run:` strings change; no code, no logic

### cons / tradeoffs

- the fix relies on the enrolled brain honoring a brief in its context (probabilistic), not a hard parse guarantee. the judge still treats an unreadable review as `malfunction` — so a stray brain can still block, but that's the correct safety behavior, not a regression.
- `claude-sonnet-5[1m]` is a heavier/pricier brain; budgets (`budget: 3`) are unchanged.

### edgecases & pit-of-success

- **role not linked**: if `reviewer` weren't in `.agent/`, enroll would silently drop it (dedup over discovered slugs). verified it IS linked (`.agent/repo=bhrain/role=reviewer/briefs/contract.reviewer-output.md` present), so we stay in the pit.
- **acceptance test**: `blackbox/role=behaver/skill.init.behavior.guards.acceptance.test.ts` stubs `enroll`/`claude` to echo success and does not assert on model or role strings (lines 250-336). adding `,reviewer` and swapping the model needs no test change.
- **model string quoting**: `[1m]` contains brackets. keep it single-quoted (`--model 'claude-sonnet-5[1m]'`) so bash does not glob-expand the brackets. the `run:` value stays a valid yaml plain scalar (already contains `'…'` around the `-p` prompt).

## open questions & assumptions

### assumptions

- **model string** = `claude-sonnet-5[1m]`, per the wisher's clarification ("its claude --model 'claude-sonnet-5[1m]'"). taken as literal truth; not independently verified against an anthropic model registry.
- **scope = templates only**. the deliverable is the init template guards (source of truth). extant `.behavior/*/…guard` instances (including this route's own) are findsert copies and are intentionally left untouched — they will not be regenerated.
- **all 11 reviewers** get `reviewer` added, including the `behaver,ergonomist,mechanic` trio in `5.3.verification.guard`. the wish says "all the enroll claude peer reviewers."
- **consumer repos link the `reviewer` role**. these templates ship from bhuild into consumer repos, where `$rhx enroll claude` runs against the consumer's `.agent/`. `,reviewer` resolves only if that consumer has `rhachet roles link`ed bhrain's reviewer role. if absent, enroll silently drops the slug (dedup over discovered roles) and the fix is a no-op there — no error, just old behavior. consumers already link `behaver/architect/mechanic/ergonomist` (the current reviewers use them), so adding `reviewer` to their link set is the natural companion step, but it is a dependency outside these template edits.

### open questions (triaged)

- **[answered]** append `,reviewer` or drop an extant role? — append only, drop none. logic: the current roles (behaver/architect/mechanic/ergonomist) each supply review lenses the wisher still wants; the contract is additive, not a replacement.
- **[answered]** add `reviewer` to the **self**-reviews too? — no. self-reviews are `say:` prompts the driver runs itself, not enrolled brains; the stdout-count contract applies only to enrolled peer reviewers. verified via guard structure.
- **[answered]** does the judge actually parse enrolled-reviewer stdout (given no `--output`)? — yes. `runStoneGuardReviews.ts:173-212` captures stdout and parses counts via `getReviewCountsFromContent`. (see internal research.)
- **[wisher]** exact model slug `claude-sonnet-5[1m]` — taken from the wisher's stated value; only the wisher (or a live spawn) can confirm it resolves. blast radius if wrong = all enrolled reviewers fail, so worth a one-line confirm.
- **[wisher]** should consumer repos also link bhrain's `reviewer` role as a companion step? — the template edit is inert in a consumer that has not linked reviewer (silent drop). whether to bundle a link-reviewer note is a wisher call, outside these template edits.
- **[research]** does the single-quoted `[1m]` token survive the guard runner's exec end-to-end? — deferred to the verification stone (execution-time smoke test), not resolvable at vision stage.

## what is awkward?

- **probabilistic guarantee**: we fix a parse-reliability problem by adding a brief to the brain's context, which the brain must then honor. it's the right fix (fix-at-cause, enroll the role that owns the contract) but it's not a hard guarantee — a determined brain could still emit prose. the judge's `malfunction`-on-unparseable behavior is the backstop.
- **model string smell**: `claude-sonnet-5[1m]` with literal brackets is an unusual, fragile-looking token to embed in a yaml `run:` line. quoting handles it, but it invites future confusion ("is that a typo? a glob?").
- **template vs instance drift**: a template-only change means already-initialized routes keep the old reviewers. that's intended, but it means the improvement rolls out only to *new* routes, not retroactively.
- **we enroll the whole role for one brief**: the `reviewer` role bundles more than the contract — its boot loads `review.tactics` + `rules101.*` briefs (say), and it ships a `keyrack.yml` (`FIREWORKS_API_KEY`, env.prep) plus skills `review.sh`/`reflect.sh` that assume a fireworks brain. we want only `contract.reviewer-output` in the brain's context. this is acceptable: enroll loads role briefs/skills as *context*, and does not enforce the role's keyrack at spawn (keyrack binds only when a skill that needs the key runs, which a claude reviewer never does). the extra `rules101`/`review.tactics` briefs are complementary, not in conflict — they reinforce review quality. net: a small semantic over-reach (a fireworks-flavored role hosted on a claude brain) with no functional downside. verified `reviewer/keyrack.yml` and `reviewer/boot.yml` to confirm.
