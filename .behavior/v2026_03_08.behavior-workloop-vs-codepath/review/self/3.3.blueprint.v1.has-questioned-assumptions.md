# self-review: has-questioned-assumptions

## technical assumptions questioned

### assumption 1: factory must come before product (3.3.0 before 3.3.1)

**what do we assume without evidence?**
that tools must be declared before code design.

**what if the opposite were true?**
if product came first, mechanic might design code without consideration for how to verify it.

**is this based on evidence or habit?**
evidence: test-driven development philosophy supports tools-first. you can't verify what you don't know how to test.

**verdict**: ✅ assumption is sound.

### assumption 2: three research prefixes are needed (3.1.1, 3.1.2, 3.1.3)

**what do we assume without evidence?**
that external.product, external.factory, and internal are meaningfully distinct.

**could a simpler approach work?**
- two prefixes (external vs internal)? no — loses factory vs product distinction.
- four prefixes (split internal into code.prod vs code.test)? no — over-complicates.

**what exceptions exist?**
small behaviors might not need all three categories. but the prefixes can be empty.

**verdict**: ✅ three is the right number.
