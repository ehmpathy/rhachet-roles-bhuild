# reflection: premortem

## premortem: imagine we shipped and it failed

**scenario:** we shipped the updated repros and verification templates, and it failed badly.

1. **mechanics found it burdensome** — the extra structure felt like busy-work, so they filled it in minimally or skipped sections
2. **guards blocked but didn't help** — self-reviews prompted but mechanics just answered "yes" without real reflection
3. **snapshots caused maintenance pain** — every output change required snapshot updates, mechanics started to avoid them

---

## what obvious signs did we ignore?

- mechanics complained about route length before this change — more content may increase friction
- self-review effectiveness depends on honest reflection, which can't be forced
- snapshot maintenance is a known pain point that wasn't addressed

---

## what assumptions could turn out wrong?

| assumption | what if wrong? | likelihood |
|------------|----------------|------------|
| mechanics will fill in journey sketches thoroughly | they fill minimally, just to pass | medium |
| self-reviews prompt honest reflection | they click through without thought | medium |
| snapshots are worth the maintenance cost | mechanics avoid them, test quality drops | low |
| repros → verification linkage is clear | mechanics don't understand the connection | low |

---

## what is the nightmare scenario?

**worst outcome:** mechanics find the route so burdensome that they:
1. rush through repros with minimal sketches
2. answer self-reviews dishonestly
3. avoid snapshots entirely
4. foreman still finds gaps at PR review

result: more friction, same outcome, worse morale.

---

## what mitigations to consider?

| risk | mitigation | cost of mitigation |
|------|------------|--------------------|
| sketches feel like busy-work | include concrete examples in stone content | low — already in vision |
| self-reviews answered dishonestly | make prompts specific with questions that require evidence | low — already planned |
| snapshot maintenance burden | prescribe masks for dynamic values | low — already in vision edgecases |
| connection unclear | verification references repros by filename | low — already in implementation notes |
| route feels longer | guards are self-review only (no peer review) | already in vision mitigations |

---

## conclusion

mitigations are already addressed in the vision:
- concrete examples for CLI, app, SDK contexts
- self-review questions that require evidence
- mask guidance for dynamic values
- explicit filename reference in verification
- self-review only to reduce friction

risk profile is acceptable. proceed with implementation.
